№ 02 About The Center

From 2017 to Now: Our Origins

The Basic Needs Center opened in 2017 at the end of Lot 5. By 2023, it had grown into a fully resourced campus center and a permanent home for fourteen programs spanning food, housing, financial wellness, transportation, social work, and family support.

Our name reflects our evolution throughout the years: helping students meet the basic conditions for staying enrolled, healthy, and on track to graduate.

№ 04 CalFresh, in depth
Federal Assistance Spotlight

Understanding CalFresh.

CalFresh is California’s version of the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), the federal food-assistance program. Funds are provided through an EBT card and able to be used at most grocery stores and farmers markets. Benefits which are not used by end of the month roll over to the next month.

Max / month · 1 person$298
Cost to apply$0
Funding100% federal
Renews every6–12 mo

Eligibility Criteria:

  • California residency.
  • U.S. Citizen or Permanent Resident.
  • Income under the limit (household-based). For one-person households, the max gross monthly income is $2,610. Financial aid for tuition and most student loans do not count as income.
  • Student qualifications. Enrolled at least half-time? You also have to meet one student exemption; see the column on the right.

Students must meet one of these requirements:

  • Cal Grant A/B (TANF-Funded)
  • Federal Work Study
  • Campus Employment Program
  • Employed 20 Hours Per Week
  • TRIO/FYRE/Summer Bridge Scholars
  • Parenting Students (with child under 12)
  • Research or Teaching Assistant
  • Other Approved Academic & Professional Programs

№ 06 FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q.01 Do students have to prove financial status to use the pantry or get help?

No. The FRESH Pantry, Zot Bites, Snack Stations, and most of the food-related resources only ask for a current UCI student ID and enrollment.

However, a few specific programs do have eligibility criteria. CalFresh has income and citizenship rules. The Economic Crisis Response Grant requires documentation that other resources are exhausted. Sponsored Housing requires a campus social worker referral.

Q.02 What if I need help right now?

There are two paths for urgent situations:

  1. On Tuesdays through Thursdays from 10AM-3PM, visit the FRESH Pantry. During after hours, sign up for Zot Bites for potential food resources.
  2. For housing, an emergency meal swipe, or anything else urgent, book a same-week appointment with a Campus Social Worker (Peter or Christy) through the Basic Needs website. The social workers can provide you with Emergency Meal Swipes, refer you to Sponsored Housing, and connect you to the Economic Crisis Response Grant.

Email basicneeds@uci.edu if you are not sure where to start.
